Andrey Kotov has been fascinated by chivalric novels since childhood. He spent hours in the library, reading stories about valiant warriors, beautiful ladies and treacherous dragons. Images of mighty knights dressed in shining armor were born in his head, and he dreamed of one day creating something that would allow people to touch this magical world. ⚪ ️ ️️Andrey’s dream began to come true when he became interested in computer games. He greedily absorbed everything related to 3D modeling and design, and gradually his hobby turned into a profession. Andrey became a talented 3D artist, and his work was highly appreciated in the gaming industry. ⚪ But Andrey couldn’t forget about his childhood dream. He wanted to create not just beautiful pictures, but an interactive collection of clothes for knights that would allow people not only to see, but also to feel like a medieval warrior. And so, after many months of work, Andrey presented his collection to the world. She made a real splash! Knights of all stripes and ages were delighted with the opportunity to try on the armor created with such care and love of detail. Andrey Kotov’s collection has become a real breakthrough in the world of interactive design. She not only allowed people to touch the world of chivalry, but also inspired other artists to create their own interactive creations. Andrey is not going to stop there. In the distant kingdom of Armorium, there lived a passionate knight named Ser Gerald. He was known for his magnificent skill in battle and his dedication to the code of chivalry. But Gerald had another hobby that few people knew about. Unbeknownst to everyone, he collected armor—the majestic, elaborately crafted suits that knights wore in battle. Gerald has always dreamed of creating the most magnificent collection of armor in the whole kingdom. He strove to ensure that every piece of armor in his collection was unique and had its own history. But in order to collect such a collection, Gerald had to travel all over the kingdom, look for rare and valuable specimens, communicate with master blacksmiths and explore forgotten fortresses and dungeons, where, as legends said, ancient armor was stored. With each new acquisition, Gerald learned more about his subjects. He discovered armor that belonged to famous knights of the past, whose names had been forgotten by time. He found rare specimens decorated with inlays and carvings, in which the secrets of ancient masters were hidden.
